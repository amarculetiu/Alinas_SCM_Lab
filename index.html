Purpose: This activity trains you to apply the Kraljic Purchasing Portfolio Matrix — a foundational tool in supply chain management. You will analyse real-world sourcing scenarios for five global companies and decide where each ingredient or material sits based on two dimensions: how much it impacts the company's profit, and how risky the supply side is.

🏢 The Companies

You will work through five companies: Nestlé, Tesla, McDonald's, Starbucks, and TSMC. Each company has four sourcing scenarios — one for each quadrant of the matrix. Navigate companies using the tabs below.

📋 The Quadrants

  • Strategic: High profit impact + High supply risk → Build deep partnerships
  • Leverage: High profit impact + Low supply risk → Exploit buying power
  • Bottleneck: Low profit impact + High supply risk → Secure supply continuity
  • Non-Critical: Low profit impact + Low supply risk → Streamline & automate

⚠️ What is "Supply Risk"?

  • Geographic concentration — few countries or regions produce it
  • Market structure — few suppliers or intermediaries control trade
  • Substitutability — how hard is it to switch suppliers or find alternatives?
  • Lead times — how long does qualifying a new supplier take?
  • External shocks — political risk, climate, regulation, conflict
