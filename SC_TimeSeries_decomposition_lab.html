Demand Signal Decomposition Lab
Supply Chain Management · Time Series Analysis · Ungraded Practice Activity

Purpose: In Supply Chain Management, real demand signals are never perfectly smooth. They are made up of four hidden components. This lab teaches you to identify each one by adjusting sliders until your reconstruction matches a mystery signal.

Level — the baseline average demand Trend — steady rise or fall over time Seasonality — repeating peaks and troughs Noise — random, unpredictable variation
🎲 What does the Noise slider do?
Noise represents random variation that cannot be predicted or explained — like a sudden one-week spike from an unexpected promotion, or a dip from a delivery delay. In this lab, the noise pattern is fixed for each scenario (you cannot perfectly reproduce it), but the Noise slider controls how much random variation your model expects. Set it to roughly match how "jagged" or irregular the mystery signal looks. A smoother signal = low noise. A very spiky, erratic signal = high noise. Your overall fit score rewards getting the structural components (level, trend, seasonality) right — noise is judged separately.

How to use this lab:

1. A mystery demand signal (blue line) is shown in the chart. Study its shape — is it rising? Flat? Are there regular peaks? How jagged is it?

2. Adjust the four sliders. Your reconstruction (orange dashed line) will update live as you move them.

3. Click Check Answer to see how well each component matches. You will receive a score and specific feedback on what to fix.

4. Use Reveal Answer at any time to see the true values — no penalty. Use New Scenario or the difficulty buttons to try a different challenge.

This activity is ungraded. Your results are for your own learning and are not submitted to your instructor.
